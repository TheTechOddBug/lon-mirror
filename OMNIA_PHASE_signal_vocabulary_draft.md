**Author:** Massimiliano Brighindi

---

## Purpose

This draft defines the first minimal signal vocabulary for **OMNIA-PHASE**.

It does not yet define equations.
It does not yet define thresholds.
It does not yet define a classifier.

Its role is narrower:

- identify the dominant structural signals that characterize each phase
- reduce ambiguity in phase reading
- prepare the possibility of future transition scoring

This draft remains outside all repositories.

---

## Core principle

A phase should not be identified by a name alone.

A phase must be readable through a small set of dominant signals.

That means:

- each phase has a characteristic signal profile
- no single scalar should define the whole phase alone
- a phase is recognized by relative dominance among structural signals

This is the correct reading.

---

## Minimal signal set

The first minimal signal vocabulary is:

1. **instability**
2. **coherence**
3. **opportunity**
4. **recoverability**
5. **saturation pressure**
6. **stop pressure**

These are not yet formulas.
They are the minimum structural descriptors needed to read the current phase system.

2. Coherence

Coherence measures how strongly the process exhibits stable structural persistence and reduced internal competition.

High coherence suggests:

reduced fragmentation

more stable organization

stronger persistence under perturbation or transformation

lower contested structure


Coherence is strongest in:

---

3. Opportunity

Opportunity measures whether useful residual structural space still remains.

High opportunity suggests:

continuation still has structural value

new gain is still possible

the process is not yet exhausted

the remaining space is not merely noise


Opportunity is strongest in:

OPPORTUNITY


---

4. Recoverability

Recoverability measures whether the process can still return to a useful structural trajectory rather than remain lost, broken, or exhausted.

High recoverability suggests:

local failure is not terminal

continuation can still yield a productive state

instability has not yet become structurally fatal


Recoverability matters most in transitions involving:

BIFURCATION
OPPORTUNITY
SATURATION

It is not itself a phase. It is a condition of possible continuation.


---

5. Saturation pressure

Saturation pressure measures how strongly the process is approaching a state in which further continuation yields diminishing structural gain.

High saturation pressure suggests:

opportunity is shrinking

marginal gain is collapsing

continuation becomes less informative

the system is moving toward exhaustion


Saturation pressure is strongest in:

SATURATION


---

6. Stop pressure

Stop pressure measures how strongly the process is approaching or has reached a state where continuation is no longer justified.

High stop pressure suggests:

continuation has become structurally unjustified

recovery is absent or irrelevant

further evolution no longer yields useful signal

external stop or escalation becomes appropriate


Stop pressure is strongest in:

STOP


---

Signal relationships

These signals are not independent in the naive sense.

Some of them are naturally opposed, while others may coexist.

Typical oppositions

Instability vs coherence

These usually move in opposite directions.

high instability often implies lower coherence

high coherence usually implies reduced instability


But transitional overlap is possible.

Opportunity vs saturation pressure

These often move in opposite directions.

high opportunity suggests structural space remains

high saturation suggests that space is collapsing


Again, overlap is possible in borderline states.

Recoverability vs stop pressure

These also often oppose each other.

high recoverability means continuation may still be justified

high stop pressure means continuation may no longer be justified

Phase signal signatures

The current draft reads each phase through a dominant signature.

BIFURCATION

Dominant profile:

instability: high

coherence: low to moderate

opportunity: variable

recoverability: variable

saturation pressure: low to moderate

stop pressure: low to moderate


Interpretation: the process is splitting and local structure is contested.


---

CONSOLIDATION

Dominant profile:

instability: low

coherence: high

opportunity: moderate

recoverability: moderate to high

saturation pressure: low to moderate

stop pressure: low


Interpretation: local structure is becoming coherent and persistent.


---

OPPORTUNITY

Dominant profile:

instability: low to moderate

coherence: moderate

opportunity: high

recoverability: high

saturation pressure: low

stop pressure: low


Interpretation: continuation is still structurally worth pursuing.


---

SATURATION

Dominant profile:

instability: variable

coherence: variable

opportunity: low

recoverability: falling

saturation pressure: high

stop pressure: rising


Interpretation: the process is losing structural productivity.


---

STOP

Dominant profile:

instability: irrelevant or terminal

coherence: irrelevant or terminal

opportunity: near zero

recoverability: near zero or irrelevant

saturation pressure: high or completed

stop pressure: dominant


Interpretation: continuation is no longer justified.

---

Dominant readout rule

The current draft assumes the following principle:

a phase is identified by the dominant signal profile,
not by a single scalar in isolation

This is essential.

If later work reduces the system to one number too early, OMNIA-PHASE collapses back into oversimplification.


---

Why this vocabulary matters

Without a signal vocabulary, OMNIA-PHASE remains poetic.

With a signal vocabulary, OMNIA-PHASE becomes at least structurally readable.

This draft therefore provides:

1. a common language for the current phases


2. a bridge between modules and phase interpretation


3. a basis for future transition scoring


4. a test of whether the phase concept is actually useful




---

Immediate weaknesses

This draft remains weak because:

1. no signal is yet formally defined


2. dominance is still qualitative


3. some signals may later collapse into fewer variables


4. the five-phase system may still be too coarse or too redundant



So this remains exploratory.


---

Promotion rule

This draft should remain outside any repository until at least one of these becomes true:

signals can be scored coherently

a small synthetic phase classifier becomes possible

the vocabulary reduces ecosystem confusion instead of increasing it

the phase grammar produces genuine explanatory gain over the modular reading


Until then, it remains private.


---

Minimal conclusion

OMNIA-PHASE becomes structurally meaningful only if each phase can be read through a dominant signal profile.

This draft defines the first minimal signal vocabulary for that purpose.
