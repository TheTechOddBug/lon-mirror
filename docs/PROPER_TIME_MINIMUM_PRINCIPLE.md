# Proper-Time Minimum Principle (k)
**MB-X.01 — Technical Note**

---

## 1. Definition

Let:

\[
k > 0
\]

be defined as the **minimum physically distinguishable increment of proper time** for any timelike physical process.

---

## 2. Fundamental Principle

For any physical system evolving along a timelike worldline:

\[
\Delta \tau \ge k
\]

where:

- \(\tau\) = proper time  
- \(\Delta \tau\) = separation between physically distinguishable states  

---

## 3. Domain of Validity

This principle applies only to:

\[
\Delta s^2 > 0
\]

(timelike separations)

It does **not apply** to:

\[
\Delta s^2 = 0
\]

(null trajectories, e.g. photons)

---

## 4. Relativistic Consistency

Proper time is invariant:

\[
d\tau = dt \sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}
\]

Thus:

- \(k\) is invariant across frames  
- coordinate time transforms as:

\[
\Delta t = \frac{k}{\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
\]

No violation of Lorentz invariance arises.

---

## 5. Discrete Proper-Time Evolution

Physical evolution occurs in steps:

\[
\tau_n = n k,\quad n \in \mathbb{N}
\]

Quantum evolution can be written as:

\[
\psi_{n+1} = e^{-i\hat H k/\hbar}\,\psi_n
\]

This preserves:

- unitarity  
- probability conservation  

---

## 6. Frequency Bound

For any system:

\[
T_0 = N k,\quad N \ge 1
\]

\[
f_0 = \frac{1}{N k}
\]

Thus:

\[
f_{0,\max} \sim \frac{1}{k}
\]

---

## 7. Energy Scale

From:

\[
E \sim \hbar \omega
\]

it follows:

\[
E_{\max} \sim \frac{\hbar}{k}
\]

---

## 8. Physical Meaning

k is not:

- a coordinate time unit  
- a universal clock tick  
- a discretization of coordinate time  

k is:

> the minimum proper-time separation for physically distinguishable states along timelike processes.

---

## 9. Relation to Planck Scale

If k is universal, then:

\[
k \sim t_P = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^5}}
\]

This follows from the only available universal constants:

- \(\hbar\) (quantum constraint)  
- \(G\) (gravitational backreaction)  
- \(c\) (causal structure)  

---

## 10. Interpretation

The mathematical continuum remains valid.

However:

> physical evolution may not be infinitely distinguishable along timelike trajectories.

---

## 11. What This Does NOT Claim

This principle does not claim:

- that coordinate time is discrete  
- that photons have a minimum time step  
- that k is experimentally established  
- that k is exactly equal to Planck time  

---

## 12. What This DOES Claim

- k is a structurally consistent hypothesis  
- it is compatible with relativity (proper time invariance)  
- it is compatible with unitary quantum evolution (discrete stepping)  
- it naturally points to the Planck scale  

---

## 13. Falsifiability Criterion

The principle becomes physically meaningful only if it implies at least one necessary consequence that differs from continuous time.

Minimal criterion:

> There exists, even in principle, a regime where no physical process can resolve intervals smaller than k in proper time.

If no such consequence exists:

- k reduces to a reformulation of continuous theory

If such a consequence exists:

- k becomes a physically testable hypothesis

---

## 14. Final Statement

\[
\boxed{
\Delta \tau_{\min} = k
}
\]

where:

> k is the minimum invariant proper-time distinguishability threshold for timelike physical processes.
