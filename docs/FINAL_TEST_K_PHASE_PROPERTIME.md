# Final Test for k on Proper-Time Quantum Phase
**MB-X.01 — Final Decision Note**

---

## 1. Scope

This document defines the final conceptual test for the hypothesis:

\[
\Delta \tau_{\min} = k
\]

where \(k\) is proposed as the minimum physically distinguishable increment of proper time for timelike processes.

This test is intended to decide whether k is:

- physically meaningful  
or
- only a coherent reformulation of continuous time

---

## 2. Reason for This Test

Previous routes were insufficient:

- market-based signals were structurally irrelevant
- interpolation-based oscillator tests were too dependent on model choice
- numerical residues alone did not establish physical necessity

The final question must therefore be asked at the most sensitive level:

> does discrete proper-time stepping leave a non-removable residue in quantum phase evolution?

---

## 3. Continuous Proper-Time Phase

Take the cleanest timelike quantum evolution:

\[
\psi(\tau)=e^{-iE\tau/\hbar}
\]

where:

- \(\tau\) = proper time
- \(E\) = energy
- \(\hbar\) = reduced Planck constant

This is the standard continuous phase evolution.

---

## 4. Discrete Proper-Time Phase

If k exists, then proper time is only physically distinguishable at:

\[
\tau_n = n k,\qquad n \in \mathbb{N}
\]

Thus the evolution becomes:

\[
\psi_n = e^{-iE n k/\hbar}
\]

This is the discrete proper-time version of the same phase process.

---

## 5. Core Question

The entire hypothesis reduces to this:

> Is the discrete sequence \(\psi_n\) physically equivalent to the continuous phase \(\psi(\tau)\), or does it imply an unavoidable structural difference?

If it is always equivalent, k is redundant.

If it implies an unavoidable difference, k becomes physically meaningful.

---

## 6. First Structural Consequence

Two energies \(E\) and \(E'\) generate the same discrete phase sequence if:

\[
e^{-iEk/\hbar} = e^{-iE'k/\hbar}
\]

which implies:

\[
(E' - E)k = 2\pi m \hbar,\qquad m \in \mathbb{Z}
\]

thus:

\[
E' - E = \frac{2\pi m\hbar}{k}
\]

---

## 7. Interpretation

This means that discrete proper-time stepping induces an energy periodicity:

\[
E \sim E + \frac{2\pi\hbar}{k}
\]

This is not present in ordinary continuous-time phase evolution.

So the test becomes:

> is this periodicity physically meaningful, or only a mathematical redundancy with no observable consequence?

---

## 8. Death Condition for k

k fails if the following is true:

> all consequences of discrete proper-time phase evolution can be fully reabsorbed into the standard continuous formulation with no invariant physical residue.

In that case:

\[
k \equiv \text{sampling convention only}
\]

and has no physical necessity.

---

## 9. Survival Condition for k

k survives only if discrete proper-time phase evolution implies at least one unavoidable consequence that continuous theory does not remove.

Examples of valid survival signals:

- intrinsic phase aliasing at high energy
- non-removable periodicity in energy identification
- minimum distinguishable phase increment
- proper-time phase ambiguity that cannot be eliminated by reparameterization

If at least one of these is physically necessary, then k becomes non-redundant.

---

## 10. Minimal Invariant to Search For

The correct object is not raw numerical residue.

It is:

> an invariant difference between continuous and discrete proper-time phase structure.

This difference must be:

- frame-independent
- not interpolation-dependent
- not removable by rescaling
- not a trivial sampling artifact

---

## 11. Relation to Energy Scale

If the periodicity induced by k is physically meaningful, then it introduces a natural energy scale:

\[
E_* \sim \frac{\hbar}{k}
\]

If k is universal and Planckian, then:

\[
E_* \sim E_P
\]

where \(E_P\) is the Planck energy scale.

This does not prove k, but it places its natural domain of relevance.

---

## 12. Decision Boundary

There are only two stable outcomes.

### Outcome A — k is redundant
Discrete proper-time phase is fully equivalent to continuous phase, with no invariant residue.

Then:

\[
k \text{ is not a physical principle}
\]

### Outcome B — k is non-redundant
Discrete proper-time phase implies a structural consequence that continuous phase cannot erase.

Then:

\[
k \text{ remains a live physical hypothesis}
\]

No third stable option exists.

---

## 13. Final Criterion

The final test is passed only if this statement is true:

> Discrete proper-time phase evolution induces a necessary and invariant physical distinction from continuous proper-time phase evolution.

If this statement fails, k fails.

---

## 14. Final Statement

\[
\boxed{
k \text{ survives only if proper-time phase discreteness leaves a non-removable invariant residue.}
}
\]

Without that residue, k is coherent but unnecessary.
