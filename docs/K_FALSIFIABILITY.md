# K Falsifiability
**MB-X.01 — Technical Note**

---

## 1. Scope

This document defines the falsifiability boundary of the hypothesis:

\[
\Delta \tau_{\min} = k
\]

where \(k\) is proposed as the **minimum physically distinguishable increment of proper time** for timelike physical processes.

The purpose of this note is to prevent conceptual drift.

---

## 2. Core Hypothesis

For any timelike physical process:

\[
\Delta \tau \ge k
\]

This means:

- proper time may not be infinitely distinguishable
- physical evolution may occur only through distinguishable increments of proper time
- coordinate time remains non-fundamental

---

## 3. What Would Make k Meaningless

k is not physically meaningful if it produces no necessary difference from continuous time.

If every prediction, structure, and limit of the hypothesis can be recovered identically from standard continuous theory without loss, then:

\[
k \equiv \text{a reformulation, not a physical principle}
\]

In that case, k has no independent status.

---

## 4. Minimal Falsifiability Criterion

The hypothesis becomes physically meaningful only if it implies at least one necessary consequence that continuous time does not.

Minimal condition:

> There must exist, at least in principle, a regime in which no timelike physical process can resolve proper-time intervals below \(k\).

If no such regime exists, the hypothesis collapses into descriptive redundancy.

---

## 5. Strong Falsifiability Criterion

The hypothesis fails if at least one of the following is true.

### F1 — Arbitrarily small proper-time distinguishability remains physically possible
If a timelike system can, in principle, distinguish:

\[
\Delta \tau \to 0
\]

without contradiction, then:

\[
k = 0
\]

and the hypothesis is false.

---

### F2 — k produces no invariant consequence
If k does not imply any invariant bound on at least one of the following:

- proper-time resolution
- intrinsic frequency
- physically distinguishable state transitions
- maximum meaningful internal rate of evolution

then k is physically empty.

---

### F3 — k depends on the system, apparatus, or observer
If k is not universal but varies with:

- material
- detector
- frame
- model choice

then it is not a fundamental principle, but an effective threshold.

---

### F4 — continuous theory already contains all observable consequences with no residue
If every consequence attributed to k is exactly recoverable in standard continuous relativistic quantum theory with no additional constraint, then k has no necessity.

---

## 6. What Would Support k

The hypothesis gains support if at least one of the following holds.

### S1 — A universal lower bound on proper-time distinguishability exists
If all timelike processes share a lower bound:

\[
\Delta \tau_{\min} > 0
\]

independent of system-specific details, then k becomes physically plausible.

---

### S2 — Intrinsic frequency is universally bounded
If there exists a universal upper bound on proper-frame frequency:

\[
f_{0,\max} \sim \frac{1}{k}
\]

then k acquires direct operational meaning.

---

### S3 — High-energy distinguishability fails before continuous resolution is reached
If increasing temporal resolution requires energy such that the physical regime self-destroys before arbitrarily small \(\Delta \tau\) is reached, then k becomes structurally natural.

---

### S4 — Proper-time evolution is only effectively continuous
If continuous proper-time evolution is only an approximation to a deeper distinguishable-step process, then k becomes a candidate fundamental threshold.

---

## 7. Domain Restriction

This hypothesis applies only to:

\[
\Delta s^2 > 0
\]

That is, timelike processes with non-zero proper time.

It does **not** apply to null trajectories:

\[
\Delta s^2 = 0
\]

Therefore, photons do not falsify k, because photons do not carry proper time.

---

## 8. Relation to Relativity

k is compatible with relativity only in the following form:

- k is a minimum of **proper time**
- not a minimum of coordinate time

Thus:

\[
d\tau = dt \sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}
\]

and:

\[
\Delta t = \frac{k}{\sqrt{1-\frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
\]

for an external observer.

If k is interpreted as minimum coordinate time, the hypothesis fails immediately.

---

## 9. Relation to Quantum Theory

k remains compatible with quantum theory only if evolution is written as discrete proper-time stepping:

\[
\psi_{n+1} = e^{-i\hat H k/\hbar}\psi_n
\]

This preserves:

- unitarity
- norm conservation
- probabilistic consistency

However, if no physically necessary distinction from continuous evolution follows, the hypothesis remains redundant.

---

## 10. Relation to Planck Scale

If k is universal, then its natural scale must be constructed only from:

- \(c\)
- \(\hbar\)
- \(G\)

Thus the natural candidate is:

\[
k \sim t_P = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar G}{c^5}}
\]

This does **not** prove k.

It only states that if a universal proper-time threshold exists, its natural scale is Planckian.

---

## 11. Closed Negative Result

A market-based validation route was tested and rejected.

Specifically:

- BTC daily data was tested
- dynamic \(k(t)\) based on rolling structural distinguishability was computed
- the result collapsed to a degenerate minimum regime
- no universal or physically meaningful k emerged from that route

Conclusion:

> market time series do not provide a valid path to validate k as a fundamental physical principle.

This branch is closed.

---

## 12. Decision Boundary

There are only two logically stable outcomes.

### Outcome A — k is real
Then k must imply at least one invariant physical limit not reducible to continuous theory.

### Outcome B — k is not real
Then k is only a coherent reformulation of finite distinguishability in practice, with no ontological status.

No third stable option exists.

---

## 13. Final Criterion

The hypothesis survives only if this statement is true:

> There exists a universal lower bound on physically distinguishable proper-time separation for timelike processes, and this bound cannot be removed without destroying physical consistency at high resolution.

If this statement fails, k fails.

---

## 14. Final Statement

\[
\boxed{
k \text{ is meaningful only if continuous proper time becomes physically insufficient.}
}
\]

Without that insufficiency, k is not a principle of nature.

It is only a notation.
